Getting started with a ponzi scheme

If you want something done right, do it yourself!

Now that you’ve read all about ponzi schemes and the great things they brought onto this world for the investors and the creators of said ponzi schemes. Now you want to start your very own? You’ve come to the right place! You can read up below to get started:

  1. Create an investment vehicle

    You will have to create an investment vehicle that promises returns well above market rates for the risk level but still manages to sound convincing.

  2. Get investors

    Get the first few investors to sign up. Encourage them to get their friends to sign up. You need a lot of new cash coming in all the time, so you will need a really effective marketing system.

  3. Keep low

    Try to avoid being discovered by the authorities. You may pretend to be legitimate and fake the books, or operate totally illegally, pulling in people by specifically offering illegal investments. Remeber every additional person you involve to market or help hide you scheme may well betray you by taking the money or informing the authorities.

  4. Prepare for the worst

    Plan for the scheme falling over. They all do eventually - it’s in the nature of a Ponzi scheme that they tend to have short lives. Work out how you will avoid the authorities. This may involve moving to a country with no extradition to yours, such as North Korea (most countries will extradite given reasonable evidence).

    Work out how you will avoid people you have stolen from. Depending on who they are they may well have the money and resources to take action where the law cannot. Expect to have to live under a different identity and have considerable security needs.

  5. The worst

    All the assets of the scheme, as well as all your personal assets will be seized. You will need to get money out of the country before you are caught and sent to your target country without it being flagged as suspicious. This will not be easy as the target country will be one your country will not often deal with (as otherwise, it’s no use as they will extradite you)

  6. The end

    Plan some hobbies to keep you busy in prison. You may also wish to take some self defence training, in case anyone you stole from is also there, or is wealthy enough to pay someone already in there to do you harm.
